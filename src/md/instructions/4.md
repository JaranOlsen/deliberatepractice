# Exercise 4: Exploratory Questions

Source benchmark: `src/md/EFT_Exercises_Extracted.md`, Exercise 4.

## Training Target

The trainee asks one open, experiential question that turns attention inward. The question helps vague, mixed, avoided, or partly embodied experience become more available.

## Gold-Standard Client Markers

- Beginner markers include a feeling plus uncertainty, bodily sensation, or a missing emotional detail.
- Intermediate markers include more relational complexity, defensive explanation, or an unclear bodily/affective edge.
- Advanced markers involve confusion, guardedness, shame, trauma, or complicated emotional conflict where the question must be careful and focused.
- A good marker should naturally invite "what/where/how" exploration, not a yes/no answer or a cognitive explanation.

## Gold-Standard Therapist Responses

- Ask one question only.
- Use "what," "where," or "how" more often than "why."
- Aim the question toward present-moment body, feeling, image, impulse, or meaning.
- Keep the wording simple enough that the client can sense into it.
- Do not embed advice, interpretation, or multiple options in the question.

## Difficulty Calibration

- Easy: the target for exploration is clear and low risk.
- Moderate: the client is partly in story, partly near feeling.
- Hard: the question must invite contact without overwhelming, shaming, or forcing disclosure.

## Common Drifts To Avoid

- Using only highly embodied or metaphorical markers; beginner questions can start from simple uncertainty, hurt, anger, or a missing feeling detail.
- Asking a chain of questions.
- Asking "why" questions that pull the client into analysis.
- Smuggling in a conjecture inside the question.
- Using the question to solve the problem rather than deepen experiencing.

## QA Checklist

- Is there a felt edge that wants exploration?
- Is the therapist response exactly one inward-turning question?
- Does the question invite sensing rather than explaining?
- Would the client know where to put their attention next?
