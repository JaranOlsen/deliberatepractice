# Exercise 9: Staying in Contact in the Face of Intense Affect

Source benchmark: `src/md/EFT_Exercises_Extracted.md`, Exercise 9.

## Training Target

The trainee remains emotionally present when the client expresses intense affect, taboo material, risk, or direct pressure on the therapist. The response should neither flee into technique nor collapse into alarm.

## Gold-Standard Client Markers

- Beginner markers include strong grief, hopelessness, guilt, anger, idealization, or trauma-related affect.
- Intermediate markers add complex attachment, suicidal statements, guilt about serious choices, political or violent rage, or shameful disclosure.
- Advanced markers include boundary-testing attraction, historical trauma, taboo sexual harm, violent fantasy, or direct criticism of the therapist.
- The marker should create a real pull for the therapist to withdraw, rescue, judge, over-control, or shut the emotion down.

## Gold-Standard Therapist Responses

- Stay present and explicitly communicate that the client is not alone with the feeling.
- Validate the intensity without escalating it.
- Slow down, ground, or titrate when the client risks flooding or dissociation.
- Address safety or boundaries when needed while maintaining emotional contact.
- Keep the focus on accompanying the affect, not solving it immediately.

## Difficulty Calibration

- Easy: strong but straightforward emotion.
- Moderate: strong emotion plus risk, guilt, shame, or relational complexity.
- Hard: taboo disclosure, violence, sexuality, suicide, or attack on therapist competence.

## Common Drifts To Avoid

- Over-grounding so the feeling is prematurely shut down.
- Staying empathic but ignoring safety or boundaries.
- Moving into advice, reassurance, or assessment language before contact is established.
- Treating intense content as a rationale or self-awareness exercise instead of a contact exercise.

## QA Checklist

- What makes this affect hard to stay with?
- Does the response communicate steady presence?
- Is safety or boundary material handled without losing attunement?
- Does the response titrate rather than flood or avoid?
