# Exercise 6: Empathic Explorations

Source benchmark: `src/md/EFT_Exercises_Extracted.md`, Exercise 6.

## Training Target

The trainee stays with the client's live emotional edge and gently helps it unfold. Unlike conjecture, exploration does not reach behind what the client has shown. Unlike a pure question, it often includes a close reflection plus an invitation to notice a little more.

## Gold-Standard Client Markers

- Beginner markers contain a feeling that is present but not fully elaborated.
- Intermediate markers contain mixed emotion, relational complexity, or a process the client is beginning to notice.
- Advanced markers contain guarded, painful, or high-stakes emotion that is available but delicate.
- The client should sound close enough to feeling that a small empathic prompt could deepen contact.
- Bracketed delivery cues should point to the live emotional edge already present, such as `[Tearful]`, `[Tense and guarded]`, or `[Low voice]`, without turning the statement into a metaphor-heavy evocation or conjecture.

## Gold-Standard Therapist Responses

- Stay one small step behind or beside the client's current experience.
- Reflect what is already there, then invite a little more contact.
- Use present-focused, process-oriented language.
- Support the client to linger with emotion, bodily sense, impulse, or meaning.
- Keep the pace gentle and collaborative.

## Difficulty Calibration

- Easy: one clear feeling with room for elaboration.
- Moderate: mixed emotion or defensiveness, but the client can still follow the thread.
- Hard: shame, trauma, despair, or anger where deepening must be carefully titrated.

## Common Drifts To Avoid

- Turning beginner explorations into metaphor-heavy or hidden-depth work; keep the emotional edge simple, present, and available.
- Jumping to a hidden feeling before the client has shown it.
- Asking broad analytic questions.
- Heightening with metaphor when the skill is unfolding, not evoking.
- Moving too fast into insight, solution, or chair work.

## QA Checklist

- What is the live emotional edge?
- Does the response stay within the client's shown experience?
- Does it invite one manageable next step of contact?
- Is the pace gentle enough for the difficulty level?
