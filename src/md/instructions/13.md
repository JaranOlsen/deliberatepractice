# Local Extension: Empathic Refocusing

Source status: not a Goldman Exercise 1-12 benchmark in `src/md/EFT_Exercises_Extracted.md`. This is an app-specific, experimental skill. If it is promoted into the production skill order, it should be validated against a named EFT source on avoidance, process-guiding, and refocusing.

## Training Target

The trainee notices when the client moves away from a live emotional edge and gently invites a return without shaming the protective move.

## Client Markers

- The client nears vulnerable emotion and then shifts into analysis, joking, storytelling, problem solving, intellectualization, minimization, or topic change.
- The emotional charge drops suddenly, even though something important had just appeared.
- The client may sound more organized cognitively but less connected affectively.
- The marker should include enough of the just-lost emotional edge that the therapist can point back to it.

## Therapist Responses

- Name the protective move warmly and respectfully.
- Validate the function of the move before inviting return.
- Link back to the exact phrase, feeling, body cue, or moment that was left.
- Ask permission or make the return invitational.
- Keep the tone collaborative; do not confront the defense harshly.

## Difficulty Calibration

- Easy: obvious topic shift after mild emotion.
- Moderate: entrenched intellectualization, shame, or fear of burdening the therapist.
- Hard: trauma avoidance, dissociation risk, anger at being redirected, or strong alliance sensitivity.

## Common Drifts To Avoid

- Treating avoidance as resistance to be corrected.
- Refocusing too quickly without validating the protection.
- Losing the exact emotional thread.
- Turning the moment into a rationale instead of a gentle return.

## QA Checklist

- What emotion or phrase was just left?
- What protective strategy appeared?
- Does the response validate protection and invite return?
- Is this safe enough for the client's difficulty level?
